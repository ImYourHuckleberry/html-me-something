Step One: Pick a Race

The race you pick for your character will effect many aspects in the game, from social interactions and special abilities, to skill modifiers

Below are the races found in the Players Handbook, with a brif description following

  • Dwarf - Short but not small, Dwarves are mountain people who tend to be played a little rougher
  • Elf - Ancient civilizations and ancient civilians, the elves are an aloof people who can slip into most roles effortlessly
  • Halfling - Home is where the heart is, Halflings are creatures of comfort who usually do not come to an adventure just for the sake of it
  • Human - I mean, you're a human, so think of what thats like
  • Dragonborn - Dovakin! Dragonborn come large, in charge, and equipped with a breath weapon
  • Gnome - Not just for garden decoration, usually kind hearted and light spirited gnomes are pint sized adventurers who pack a punch
  • Half-Elf - An outsider to both Elf and Human communities, Half-Elves face discrimination, but make up for it with charm
  • Half-Orc - An outsidet to every community, be prepared to be the outcast in every situation, and be prepared to solve most of those situations with your fists
  • Tiefling - It a cat, its Satan, its a Tiefling. Long ago Tieflings had their blood merged with an infernal power and the descendants bare the mark, youre gonna attract some stares

Step Two: Pick a Class

I see you dont have any class (HA!)

When you pick a class you pick what type of adventurer you are going to be

  • Barbarian - Solves problems with percussive persuasion
  • Bard - So good at jamming its like magic, make great faces for the party
  • Cleric - Not just healers anymore, using the power of prayer the cleric class can do anything from play doctor to play Thor
  • Druid - Its like, we're all one with nature, man. Also, I can turn into a bear
  • Fighter - Really good at attacking, then attacking, then attacking again
  • Monk - Get your Xi flow right, unarmored, barehanded, melee/magic fun
  • Paladin - Good is not nice, holy warrior out to do the work of god
  • Ranger - Do you want an animal companion? This is how you get an animal companion
  • Rogue - Sneak, sneak, sneak. Stab.
  • Sorcerer - I've got this feeling inside my bones, Sorcerers cast with innate ability
  • Warlock - Lets make a deal, Warlocks draw their power from an otherworldly patron
  • Wizard - I'm a what? You're a wizard and you get your magic from books and scrolls.

Step Three: Your Ability Scores and You

Ability Scores are your characters base stats that determine chances of success for a given action

In DnD 5th Edition there are six ability scores that make up the bulk of your charater

  • Stregnth - Ability associted with feaths of brawn and muscle. Necessary for heavy armor and swinging big weapons.
  • Constitution - Effects your base health. How big of a hit can you take? How long can you hold your breath? Ever wonder of you could survive a good poisioning?
  • Dexterity - Speed and reflexivness. Effects most armor bonuses (excluding heavy), and can influence how well dexterity weapons can be used.
  • Wisdom - Effects the perception of the character. Did you notice the feet behind the curtain? The book on the book case that is just a little out of place?
  • Intelligence - Book smarts and such. You may not be street wise but if it can be read in a book you've read it.
  • Charisma - Charm and persuasion. A character with high charisma can charm their way out of a situation that others may find themselves getting bloody over.

Because this is your first game we are going to divide up scores in the most simple way. The basic point spread

In what ever order you decide place these six scores: 15, 14, 13, 12, 10, 8

Keep in mind that the adverage person has a "10" for most abilities, so you will be above adverage for most, and below adverage for one ability.

Step Four: Create a Backstory

It's time to throw some meat on your characters bones.

Did you come up on the streets? WEre you born into a noble house, but are now cast aside? Are you a Holy Warrior who has become dissillusioned?

This is one of the best parts of DnD, you can be anything you want to be. So take a while, and really consider whose skin you want to live in while you play this game.

Playing Dress Up

Finally, you need to outfit your character with some gear.

Here is where you decide if you are playing with a sword and sheild, or a two-handed Great Axe. If you are going to use a staff or a wand. If you are going to wear full plate mail or just some leather straps

There are pros and cons to everything so pick wisely. That heavy armor is hard to sneak in, and that bastard sword takes up one too many hands for a sheild.
